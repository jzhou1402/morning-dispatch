Anthropic Announces Claude 5 With Extended Reasoning, Sets New Benchmark in AI Capability
The new model family introduces persistent memory across sessions and a 2M token context window, pushing the frontier further than analysts expected.
From: Anthropic News <news@anthropic.com>  ·  Received 11:04 PM

Anthropic released Claude 5 late Monday evening, a model the company claims outperforms every prior benchmark across reasoning, coding, and multimodal tasks. The announcement, made via email to developers enrolled in the early-access program, outlined a new architecture built around extended chain-of-thought processing.

Perhaps most notably, the new model ships with native persistent memory, allowing it to retain context across conversations without requiring users to re-supply background information. Early testers described the capability as transformative for long-running engineering projects.

The 2-million-token context window — twice the prior maximum — means developers can now pass entire codebases, research corpora, or legal documents in a single prompt without chunking or retrieval pipelines. Pricing for the new tier has not yet been disclosed.

Industry reaction was swift. Within hours of the announcement, three major cloud providers had published integration guides. Analysts at Sequoia Capital called it "the most significant model release since GPT-4." Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei noted in an accompanying blog post that safety evaluations for Claude 5 took twice as long as any prior release.
